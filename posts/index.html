    a proof of “life is unpredictable”

    October 20, 2021
    Idea
    Scheme, Lisp

    Wanna known if a person’s life is predictable. Suppose there is a procedure like this: \( P(l\quad p)=\begin{cases} true,\quad life \ is \ predictable &\\ false, \quad life \ is \ unpredictable & \end{cases} \) and we got a procedure to test if it is correct:

    (define diag                
  (lambda (p)              
    (if (P p p)              
        (other-than (p p))    
        false)))

    where other-than’s definition is:

    (define other-than
  (lambda (x)
    (if (eq? x 'a)
        'b
        'a)))

    other-than is always output the opposite of input. so if applied diag to itself—(diag diag), and (P diag diag) get two possible value: if a person’s life is predictable, then other-than get the conflicting result; if a person’s life is unpredictable, then we get a false, so it is predicatable, which is also a conflict. Then we get to known life is unpredictable.
